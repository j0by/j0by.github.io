In school, all of us were taught that hydrogen sulphide smells like rotten eggs. And most of us have experienced the same. But really, is that correct? The question we should all be asking is, Why do rotten eggs smell of hydrogen sulphide?. Hydrogen sulphide actually smells of, well, er hydrogen sulphide. And when eggs turn rotten, it produces hydrogen sulphide.

Why the chemistry lesson you ask? What I just described is something we can call attribution error. You are exposed to rotten eggs before you come to know about hydrogen sulphide. So, you attribute the smell of hydrogen sulphide to rotten eggs while in truth, hydrogen sulphide just smells of itself.
